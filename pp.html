Superposition in Quantum Computing

Level: Beginner

Estimated Study Time: 20–25 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what superposition means
  • Differentiate between classical bits and quantum states
  • Explain how a qubit can exist in multiple states simultaneously
  • Describe what happens when a quantum state is measured

Introduction

Superposition is one of the fundamental principles of quantum mechanics and the core reason why quantum computing differs from classical computing.

In classical systems, a bit can only exist in one of two states: 0 or 1. However, a quantum bit (qubit) can exist in a combination of both states at the same time. This ability is known as superposition.

Classical vs Quantum State

Classical Bit

  • Can be either 0 or 1
  • Cannot exist in both states simultaneously

Example: A light switch is either ON or OFF.

Quantum Bit (Qubit)

  • Can be 0
  • Can be 1
  • Can be a combination of 0 and 1
|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩

Where |α|² + |β|² = 1

Real-World Analogy

A spinning coin explains superposition well. When a coin lies flat, it is either heads or tails. When spinning, it is in a combination state.

Measurement and Collapse

  • It collapses into either 0 or 1
  • The probability depends on α and β

Before measurement → multiple possibilities After measurement → single definite state

Why Superposition is Important

  • Represents multiple possibilities simultaneously
  • Enables theoretical parallel computation
  • Foundation for quantum algorithms
